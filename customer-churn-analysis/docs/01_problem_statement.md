# Problem Statement

Customer churn is a critical challenge for subscription-based businesses, as acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones. **High churn rates** directly impact **revenue, profitability, and long-term growth**.

This project focuses on analyzing customer-level data from the **IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset** to understand the factors contributing to customer attrition. The dataset contains information related to customer demographics, subscribed services, billing information, and churn status.

The primary objective of this analysis is to **identify key drivers of customer churn** and provide **actionable insights** that can support data-driven customer retention strategies.

## Problems We Will Solve


### Executive & Business Overview Questions

1. What is the **overall customer churn rate**?
2. How does churn vary by **customer tenure** (new vs long-term customers)?
3. What percentage of **total revenue is at risk due to churn**?
4. How does churn trend differ across **monthly vs yearly contracts**?
5. Which customer segments (based on tenure and contract) contribute **most to churn volume**?

### Demographic & Customer Profile Analysis
Try to understand **who is churning?**

1. Does churn vary by **gender**?
2. Are **senior citizens** more likely to churn?
3. How does churn differ for customers with **partners or dependents**?
4. Which demographic group has the **highest churn probability**?
5. Is there a demographic segment that is **low churn but high revenue**?

<br/>

### Service Usage & Product Impact Analysis

1. How does churn vary by **internet service type** (DSL, Fiber, No Internet)?
2. Do customers with **fiber optic** internet churn more?
3. How do **additional services** (OnlineSecurity, TechSupport, Streaming) impact churn?

3. How does churn vary by **payment method**?

5. Is higher **monthly charge** associated with higher churn?
6. How does churn differ across **billing paperless vs non-paperless** customers?

4. Is early-stage churn responsible for **most customer loss**?
